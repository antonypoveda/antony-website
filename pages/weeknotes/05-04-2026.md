## Work week

Spent the week trying to get things in place as I expected to start 4 weeks of paternity leave at any moment from next week.

Mostly this meant:

* a lot of discussions and planning of what would be possible in the next quarter with reduced team capacity (we also have vacant roles)
* sharing useful existing documentation, like our team playbook and design hypotheses, with our new product manager
* checking that new content designer knows where things are at with the framework content for the new profession so they can run with it from now on
* helping new delivery manager run our monthly governance meeting and set up the voting process for two proposals using [GOV.UK Forms](https://www.forms.service.gov.uk/) and [Notify](https://www.notifications.service.gov.uk/)
* some of my main job - trying to do what I said I would do for each of the working groups currently drafting new descriptions, either:  
	* scheduling co-writing sessions for when I'm back 
	* editing draft content ready for wider community fact-checking in April

All these were accomplished to varying levels of success (in the order above, from 'most sorted' to 'least sorted') and none were completed. This is probably because I was attempting them all at once. So it goes.

A lot of my thinking was, "it's ok, I can finish this, this *and* this on Monday".

But there's a lot of 'new' next to those roles in my team, so I was feeling the pressure to set people up for success in case I went off suddenly.

Well that Monday morning brought our new baby daughter, 10 days 'early'.

And I think everything's going to be fine.

See y'all in 4 weeks!
